This list will contain my favorite bands and musicians

Name: Michael Jackson

Biography: Michael Jackson, born on August 29, 1958, in Gary, Indiana, was an American singer, songwriter, and dancer known as the "King of Pop." He started his music career at a young age with his brothers in the Jackson 5, quickly becoming the group's lead singer. His solo career took off in the 1970s, with groundbreaking albums like Off the Wall (1979), Thriller (1982), and Bad (1987). Thriller remains the best-selling album of all time.

Most representative album: Thriller

Name: Bruno Mars

Biography: Bruno Mars, born Peter Gene Hernandez on October 8, 1985, in Honolulu, Hawaii, is an American singer, songwriter, and producer. He grew up in a musical family and began performing at a young age. After moving to Los Angeles, he gained recognition for his songwriting skills before breaking out as a solo artist with his debut album Doo-Wops & Hooligans (2010). Known for his eclectic style blending pop, funk, R&B, and reggae, Mars has produced hits like "Just the Way You Are," "Locked Out of Heaven," and "Uptown Funk." He has won multiple Grammy Awards and is celebrated for his energetic performances and smooth vocals.

Most representative album: 24K Magic

Name: Drake

Biography: Drake, born Aubrey Drake Graham on October 24, 1986, in Toronto, Canada, is a globally renowned rapper, singer, songwriter, and actor. He initially gained recognition as an actor on the Canadian teen drama Degrassi: The Next Generation. However, his music career took off with his early mixtapes, particularly So Far Gone (2009), which produced hit singles like "Best I Ever Had" and "Successful.

Most representative album: Take care

Name: The weeknd

Biography: The Weeknd, born Abel Makkonen Tesfaye on February 16, 1990, in Toronto, Canada, is a Grammy Award-winning singer, songwriter, and record producer. Known for his genre-blending music that fuses R&B, pop, and electronic elements, The Weeknd rose to fame in the early 2010s with his mixtapes House of Balloons, Thursday, and Echoes of Silence. These works garnered critical acclaim for their moody, atmospheric sound. His first major label album, Kiss Land (2013), was followed by commercial successes like Beauty Behind the Madness (2015), which included hits like "Can't Feel My Face" and "The Hills." He further solidified his status with albums such as Starboy (2016) and After Hours (2020), featuring the record-breaking single "Blinding Lights.

Most representative album: After Hours

Name: Queen

Biography:Queen is a British rock band formed in London in 1970, consisting of Freddie Mercury (lead vocals, piano), Brian May (guitar), Roger Taylor (drums), and John Deacon (bass). Known for their eclectic musical style, blending rock, opera, and progressive elements, Queen became one of the most iconic bands in music history. Their theatrical live performances and Mercury's charismatic presence made them global superstars. Hits like "Bohemian Rhapsody," "We Will Rock You," and "Somebody to Love" helped define their legacy. The band continued to evolve throughout the decades and remains influential even after Mercury's passing in 1991.

Most representative album: A Night at the Opera

Name: Kendrick Lamar

Biography:Kendrick Lamar, born Kendrick Lamar Duckworth on June 17, 1987, in Compton, California, is one of the most critically acclaimed and influential rappers of his generation. He gained widespread recognition with the release of his debut studio album Section.80 (2011), which showcased his lyrical talent and socially conscious themes. His breakthrough came with good kid, m.A.A.d city (2012), a concept album that portrayed his experiences growing up in Compton. The album was praised for its storytelling and deep exploration of gang culture, violence, and survival.

Most representative album: To Pimp a Butterfly
